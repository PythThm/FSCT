#### 1. Purpose of Sentencing 
- protect the society and to do crime prevention 
- when a judge crafting a sentencing, they make it appropriate to that offence
- trying to effect these purpose the sentence are to give
- some are more important based on the offence
- its all balanced out in specific cases
- these are the back and bones of the purpose of sentencing
1. sub a 
	- denunciation
	- give a message through the sentence
	- basic code of value in society
	- court shows the public and sends the message to the community
	- the worse offence it is, the more the court need to "push it" away 
2. sub b 
	- to deter other people from committing the offence
	- doing fent, don't do it, its bad, you get jailed
3. sub c 
	- seperation from the society
	- jail is the last resort
4. sub d
	- attempt to bring wholeness back to the society
	- try and help this offender to not do this again
	- what if they come from a bad background
	- how to help them
	- probation order to help them
5. sub e
	- reparation for harm done to victim and community
	- community works, get them to contribute to the society
	- financial losses, give back the money bitch ass nigga
6. sub f
	- promote a sense of responsibility in offenders
	- acknowledge the harm done
	- restorative principles from d, e, f

#### 2. Principle
- guideline of when to make a sentencing more harsh or not
- "A sentence must be proportionate to the gravity of the offence and the degree of responsibility of the offender."
- gravity of the offence, pushing someone over vs murdering a child
- moral culpability of that person
- degree of responsibility of the offence, stealing because you are starving or out of spite

# Tools of sentencing
- different sanctions, stacking on each other

#### 1. discharge [s.730](https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/C-46/page-123.html#docCont)
	1. conditional or absolute
	2. no need for probation, go on for your life, but the thing stays on your criminal record for a year
	3. conditional is placed for probation up to 3 years, temporary criminal record
	4. cannot be discharge if there is a minimal punishment or 14 years or more for life sentences
	5. if too serious, ofc no discharge
	6. is it contrary to the public interest? question to be asked to themselves by the judge
	7. sub 3: offender shall not be deemed not to have convicted 
#### 2. Probation s.731, s.732
	1. can get stacked on top of other sentencing
	2. restrict your ability of doing stuff in the society
	3. rehab centre, help them to not repeat the same offence
	4. judge can give fine and probation, or jail time with probation
	5. s.732.2(2b) up to 3 years
	6. sub 3: addition condition to probation
	7. s.733.1: if preach probation, hybrid offences
	8. s.732.1-2 required condition, s.732.1-3 optional condition
	9. s.732.2-5 bring you back court, take back the suspended sentence and put you in jail
	10. permanent criminal record, but you can apply to get it off your record, not guarentee and often not approve
#### 3. Fines s.734
	1. can be stacked or just stand alone
	2. in addition to other sansaction if the court is required to 
	3. s.734-2 is when there is a minimum fine, like DUI fine
	4. no more less if there is a minimum fine, so no discharge no probation
	5. the judge msut be satisfy that offender can pay the fine
	6. meant to punish people' wallet 
	7. if they cant pay but minimum fine, can offer to spend time in jail in compensation of not able to pay, volunteer not forced
	8. can transfer from criminal to civil court with different judgement
	9. will be in the criminal record
#### 4. Conditional Sentence order s.742
	1. jail sentence served in the community
	2. base line is house arrest
	3. minimal 1 day long and less than 2 years
	4. put on conditions that something you cant do in jail, like drinking
	5. the purpose is to maintain the relation to the community of the offender
	6. to those who would not endanger the safety of the community
	7. judge impose imprisonment for less than 2 years, but conditional sentence under s.742.1
	8. s.742.6 (9) preaching conditional sentences, you are not presumed innocent
	9. you can despute the charge, but no trial just argue infront of the judge and convince them of what happened 
	10. not a trial but a hearing, less harsh
#### 5. Intermittent Sentence s.732
	1. where its 90 days or less
	2. when people have jobs
	3. serving jail time in chunks, so they are still connected to community
	4. if theres an appropraite accomodation, intermittent sentence can be applied 
	5. s.732(2) can apply to just jail jail
#### 6. IMPRISONMENT s.743
	1. if indictable, with no punishment provided max jail time is 5 years
	2. s.743.1(1) shit to sentence to prison in penitentiary
	3. s.743.21(1) no communications with the victim
	4. s.743.12 right to not subjecteed to cruel and unusual treatment or punishment
	5. can apply that the judge have sentenced wrongfully, CCR section 12
