# What is Crown Counsel
- Crown prosecutors, Lawyers who represent the government in criminal cases
- Works for Ministry of the Attorney General, in BC that's BC Prosecution Service (BSPS)
- They don't seek to convict, but to ensure justice is done
- They presents the case against the accused on behalf of society
- Crown Counsel are responsible for presenting the prosecution side of the matter
- Call witnesses to the stand so they can testify in court, present evidence and explain to judge and jury
- To not obtain a conviction at all cost, ensure trial process is fair to all
- Ensure evidence presented thoroughly and accurately, and the integrity of the justice process is maintained
[Role of Crown Counsel](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/justice/criminal-justice/bc-prosecution-service/about/crown-counsel)

# Informations and Indictments
#### 1.  Informations
- Initial documents that commences a criminal proceeding
- BCPC
- For summary offences
- if a case is move to superior court, the information is replaced by an indictment
#### 2. Indictment
- A formal charging document for serious offences
- BCSC
- Indictable offences
- Allows for more complex process and can involve a preliminary inquiry before a trial in superior court
