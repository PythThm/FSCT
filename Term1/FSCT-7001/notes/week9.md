# Bail
- s 515
- the justice shall release on bail without conditions or if the prosecutor prove why the accused should not get release or should release with condition
- s.515.2 is the amount of money, s.515.4 is the condition
- most release order are on s.515.2a, just court order 
- s.515.2c, someone take you accountable like your family, that person should be ensured to be trusted by the society, if sureties does not hold the accused accountable, then the court (crown counsel) can (dont have to) force them to sell properties to pay the fine
- **Sureties**: they may have to place capital on the line in case they fail to ensure the accused attends courts and respects the conditions of their bail.
- s.515.2d bail order to the court, deposit money in the court registry, only ask for deposit if they cant pay it 
- s.515.4 conditions to attach to s.515.2, similar to police release order

#### Justification for detention in custody
- one of the three concerns
- judge will think if there is risk of one of these three of happening is so high they shall not release 
- a. Primary ground: if the judge found whatever they do and accused not going to show up, they can detention
- have there been a occurrence they didn't show up to court, or just tourist and never come back
- b. Secondary ground: for safety or protection of the public, if you let them out they would commit more offences
- c. if release, will the public lose confidence for the justice system
	- If the case is very strong
	- gravity of the offence
	- firearm
	- punishment of 3 years or more imprisonment
