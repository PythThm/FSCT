# Types of evidence
 **2 main types of evidence are direct and circumstantial**
## Direct Evidence
- trying to prove or disprove evidence
- direct experience to prove what that witness is called to prove 
- example: Accused murder someone, if witness say if they are in the room with accused and he saw this guy kill another guy, this is direct experience and direct evidence
- they saw the thing that was trying to be prove
- there are no blanks needs to be filled for their evidence 
- Rest upon the credibility of the witness 
## Circumstantial
- where the witness did not directly experience of the incident
- like where they were in another room when someone else "shoot" someone else
- filling blanks of what has happened 
- potential fact of something happened 
- more circumstantial evidence we get around one evidence so the trier of fact can get closer to the truth 
## Testimonial Evidence
- most of the evidence are these evidence

## Recorded Evidence
- from your phone, camera, CCTV
- if there are footage of bob shooting alan, then the recorded evidence is direct evidence
- if there is footage of bob dragging alan's body, then its a circumstantial evidence (no direct prove of the criminal act)
## Admission
- Accused just say they are guilty and say what they did 
- prove of giving up on something
- making the proceeding more efficient

# Concept of Weight

- how much faith, how much power to prove or dis-prove
- in order to evaluate an evidence's value its comes down to weight
- linked to the credibility to the witness
- whether an evidence is admissible is what we deal with first, then weight
- admissibility does not relate to weight, just because low weight does not mean its not admissible
