# Hearsay
- witness under oath being examinated, saying this person told me something 
- general rule is hearsay is inadmissible
- state of mind or statement out of court - what they think, just putting it forward but not as a conclusional evidence
- Judge would give jury a warning, the only reason to hear that statement is to understand what the witness's situation is (or what they were thinking leading to what they did)
- one thing lawyers says to their client is DO NOT TELL ANYONE ABOUT ANYTHING
- anything the accused says can be brought to court from other people that is being summoned to court 

## [R. v. Evans, 1993 CanLII 86 (SCC)](https://www.canlii.org/en/ca/scc/doc/1993/1993canlii86/1993canlii86.html?resultId=ebcadd24826a4d1ba6dc96db5e10d0e1&searchId=2026-02-27T09:01:18:078/61374a59e0ec47e9afff9c73369d5263)
- crown says hearsay
- accused say they did not say anything like that 
- trier of fact access weight to them 
- general rule if you want to argue hearsay is admissible, look into common law exceptions
- principle exception of hearsay: prove the evidence is necessary and reliable
- if the judge says it is admissible in voir dier, then continue with trial 
- allows you to argue any hearsay as admissible
- hearsay can be anything being put in court, any form of statement
- the weight of the statement of how and why it is made of being reliable or not 

## [R. v. Khan, 1990 CanLII 77 (SCC)](https://www.canlii.org/en/ca/scc/doc/1990/1990canlii77/1990canlii77.html?resultId=2cff3b3f96c049df9fda2182b6289531&searchId=2026-02-27T09:34:43:985/49a6699839774295a380f6225309c6ab)
- reliable exception 
- supreme court says the rule is too strict, giving the principle of hearsay rule to a child 
- hearsay of a child, principle exception of hearsay is to everyone, later down the line
- context: 
	- Dr.Khan touch the kid, the mom and kid later have the convo about it
	- the judge found the child not competent to give evidence and they only have statement of the mom
	- its hearsay from the mom's statement
	- this got appealed in supreme court, says the trial judge was right
	- needing a hearsay statement from a child to show the evidence is necessary 
	- and in this case it is reliable as well based on the circumstantial 
	- ***It was also reliable; the child had no motive to falsify her story, which emerged naturally and without prompting, and the fact she could not be expected to have knowledge of such sexual acts imbues her statement with its own peculiar stamp of reliability.***
	- the real evidence (semen on her sleeve) gives the child trustworthyness 

## [R. v. Khelawon, 2006 SCC 57](https://www.canlii.org/en/ca/scc/doc/2006/2006scc57/2006scc57.html?resultId=d827703702544a63a9eb1d17b590ddcd&searchId=2026-02-27T09:47:01:204/0270b76987ac470898019007e564b113)
